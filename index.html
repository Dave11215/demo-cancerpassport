‚ùå Myth

"I'll just get a placebo with no real treatment"

‚úì Reality

Cancer trials rarely use placebos alone. You'll typically receive at least the standard treatment, often with the new therapy added.8

Costs: The study treatment is usually free. Insurance typically covers routine care, just as it would outside the trial.9

Time: Varies by study. Your team will explain the schedule before you decide.10

üìä Impact

96%

of cancer patients in trials say they would recommend participation to others facing similar decisions11

Recent breakthroughs like immunotherapy and targeted treatments came from clinical trials. Participants helped make these life-saving options available.12,13

üó£Ô∏è Questions to Ask

  • What is the purpose of this study?
  • What are my other treatment options?
  • What are the possible risks and benefits for me?
  • What will my schedule look like?
  • What costs might I have?
  • Can I still see my regular doctor?
  • What happens if I want to stop?

üåç Diversity Matters

Trials need participants from all backgrounds to ensure treatments work for everyone. If you're from an underrepresented group, your participation is especially valuable for advancing equitable care.14,15

This is your decision. Take time to learn about your options and discuss them with your care team and loved ones. There's no pressure to decide right away.
